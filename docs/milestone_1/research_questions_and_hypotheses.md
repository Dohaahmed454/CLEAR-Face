Research Questions and Hypotheses
RQ1

Can CARE choose useful corrective actions better than generic retry, fixed rules, uncertainty-only selection, random action, or immediate abstention?

Expected outcome:
CARE achieves higher recovery and lower post-action error at equal prompt burden.

RQ2

Does modelling burden and latency reduce unnecessary prompts without materially increasing risk?

Expected outcome:
Full CARE uses fewer unnecessary or repeated prompts than no-cost CARE.

RQ3

How well does a synthetic-trained policy transfer to real interactions, and how much real adaptation is needed?

Expected outcome:
Synthetic pretraining plus limited real adaptation outperforms synthetic-only and real-only training when real data are limited.

RQ4

Do complementary XAI methods provide more faithful and stable evidence than a single heatmap?

Expected outcome:
Combined evidence improves faithfulness, stability, and pre/post reacquisition analysis.

RQ5

Can structured language accurately explain predictions, requests, ambiguity, and abstention?

Expected outcome:
Grounded generation yields fewer unsupported and contradictory statements than unconstrained generation.
